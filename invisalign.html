Clear Aligner Treatment for Adults & Teens

Invisalign uses a series of custom-made, clear aligner trays to gently move teeth into their ideal position. Experience virtually invisible, comfortable, and removable orthodontic treatment that fits your lifestyle.

How Invisalign Works

Advanced 3D technology creates a personalized treatment plan that maps your tooth movement from start to finish.

Invisalign Treatment Process

You'll wear a series of custom-made, removable aligners that apply gentle, consistent pressure to move teeth. Most patients switch to a new set every 1–2 weeks, wearing aligners 20–22 hours per day.

Digital Planning

We map your tooth movement and preview your result before you start using advanced 3D imaging technology.

Removable Trays

Take aligners out to eat, drink anything besides water, and brush or floss normally.

Smart Features

Many plans use small tooth-colored "attachments" that help guide precise movement for optimal results.

Treatment Timeline & Process

Understanding what to expect during your Invisalign journey from consultation to completion.

Treatment Duration

Exact timing depends on your goals and case complexity. Many adult cases finish in about 12–18 months with consistent wear; simpler cases can be shorter.

  • Average treatment: 12-18 months
  • Simple cases: 6-12 months
  • Complex cases: 18-24 months
  • Progress monitored at regular visits

Step-by-Step Process

First, we capture digital impressions and create a personalized treatment plan. You'll wear each set of aligners for about two weeks, removing them only to eat, drink, brush and floss.

  • Initial consultation and 3D scan
  • Custom treatment plan creation
  • Aligner fabrication and delivery
  • Regular progress checkups
  • Retainer fitting after completion

Daily Routine

Wear Time

20–22 hours daily; change trays as directed for optimal progress.

Regular Visits

Periodic check-ins to confirm tracking, comfort, and treatment progress.

Retention Phase

After treatment, retainers keep your new smile stable and prevent relapse.

Invisalign vs. Traditional Braces

Understanding the differences to help you choose the best orthodontic treatment for your needs.

Invisalign Clear Aligners

Invisalign Advantages

Invisalign aligners are smooth and removable, making them a popular choice for adults and teens seeking a discreet alternative to metal brackets.

  • Virtually invisible appearance
  • Removable for eating and cleaning
  • No dietary restrictions
  • Comfortable smooth plastic
  • Fewer emergency visits
Traditional Metal Braces

When Braces Are Better

Traditional braces remain the best option for severe crowding, large gaps or complex jaw discrepancies. During your consultation, we'll evaluate your bite and recommend the most effective approach.

  • Handle complex movements
  • Work for severe crowding
  • Address jaw alignment issues
  • No compliance concerns
  • Often more cost-effective

Care & Maintenance

Proper care ensures your aligners stay clean, clear, and effective throughout treatment.

Daily Care Routine

  • Rinse aligners and brush them gently each time you brush your teeth
  • Avoid hot water and colored mouthwashes that can warp or stain trays
  • Store aligners in their case when not wearing them
  • Clean with special aligner cleaning products when needed
  • Maintain excellent oral hygiene throughout treatment

Treatment Success Tips

One of the main challenges of clear aligner therapy is compliance. The key to successful treatment is consistent wear and following your treatment plan exactly as prescribed.

  • Wear aligners 20-22 hours per day
  • Change to new aligners on schedule
  • Attend all scheduled appointments
  • Contact us immediately if aligners are lost or damaged

Who's a Good Candidate?

Invisalign can address many orthodontic concerns, but the best way to know if it's right for you is through a consultation.

Ideal Invisalign Cases

Clear aligners can address many mild to moderate spacing, crowding, and bite concerns effectively and comfortably.

  • Mild to moderate crowding
  • Spacing and gaps between teeth
  • Minor bite irregularities
  • Relapsed orthodontic cases
  • Adult and teen patients

When Other Options May Be Better

Some complex cases—especially those requiring extractions or jaw correction—may be better served with other orthodontic approaches. We'll advise you at your consultation.

  • Severe crowding requiring extractions
  • Complex jaw alignment issues
  • Significant bite problems
  • Patients who can't commit to wearing aligners
  • Certain tooth rotation cases

Why Dallas Patients Love Invisalign

Because the trays are nearly invisible and removable, you can straighten your teeth without the look or dietary restrictions of traditional braces. However, aligners must be worn consistently — about 20–22 hours per day — to be effective.
