Protect Your Orthodontic Investment

After completing orthodontic treatment, retainers are essential to maintain your beautiful new smile. Without proper retention, teeth can shift back toward their original positions, undoing years of orthodontic work.

Why Retainers Are Essential

Understanding the importance of retainers in maintaining your orthodontic results long-term.

After orthodontic treatment, your teeth, gums, and bones need time to stabilize in their new positions. During this critical period, retainers hold your teeth in place while the surrounding tissues adapt and strengthen.

What Happens Without Retainers

  • Orthodontic relapse - teeth gradually shift back toward original positions
  • Crowding returns, especially in front teeth
  • Bite changes that can affect jaw function
  • Years of orthodontic treatment become undone
  • Additional costs may be required for retreatment

Important Fact: Studies show that up to 50% of people experience some degree of orthodontic relapse without proper retainer use. The good news is that consistent retainer wear virtually eliminates this risk.

Types of Retainers

We offer several types of retainers to fit your lifestyle and maintenance preferences.

Clear Plastic Retainers

Clear Plastic Retainers (Essix)

Custom-molded clear plastic retainers that fit snugly over your teeth. Nearly invisible and comfortable to wear.

Cost

$100 - $300 per set

Lifespan

1-3 years typically

  • Nearly invisible appearance
  • Easy to clean and maintain
  • Comfortable fit
  • Affordable to replace

Considerations

  • Can crack or break with wear
  • May stain over time
  • Need replacement every 1-3 years
  • Must remove for eating
Hawley Retainers

Hawley Retainers (Wire & Acrylic)

Traditional retainers with a plastic base and wire framework. Highly durable and adjustable by your orthodontist.

Cost

$150 - $350 per retainer

Lifespan

5-10 years typically

  • Very durable construction
  • Adjustable if needed
  • Long lifespan
  • Can be personalized with colors

Considerations

  • More visible than clear options
  • Speech adjustment period
  • Bulkier design
  • Higher upfront cost
Permanent Retainers

Permanent Retainers (Fixed/Bonded)

Thin wire bonded to the back of your front teeth. Provides 24/7 retention without removable appliances.

Cost

$200 - $600 per arch

Lifespan

10-20 years typically

  • No compliance needed
  • Invisible from front view
  • Long-term solution
  • Excellent retention

Considerations

  • Harder to clean around
  • Can break or debond
  • Requires special flossing technique
  • Professional removal needed

Daily Care & Cleaning

Proper care ensures your retainers stay clean, clear, and effective throughout their lifespan.

Daily Care Routine - Do

  • Rinse retainers with cool water after removal
  • Brush gently with a soft toothbrush (no abrasive toothpaste)
  • Soak weekly in a retainer/aligner cleaner as directed
  • Store in a ventilated case when not in use
  • Keep retainers moist when not wearing

What to Avoid - Don't

  • Use hot water (can warp plastic)
  • Use bleach, alcohol, or harsh chemicals
  • Wrap in napkins (easy to throw away by accident)
  • Leave within reach of pets
  • Use abrasive toothpaste or hard brushes

Pro Tips for Retainer Care

Clean your retainers every time you brush your teeth to prevent bacteria buildup and odors. Consider using a separate toothbrush designated only for retainer cleaning.

Wearing Schedule & Replacements

Understanding the timeline for retainer wear and when replacements are needed.

Retainer Wearing Timeline

First 6 Months

Full-time wear (22 hours/day) unless instructed otherwise. Remove only for eating and cleaning.

6—12 Months

Transition to nighttime-only wear as advised. Continue excellent oral hygiene and regular checkups.

Beyond 12 Months

Nighttime wear long-term to prevent relapse. Replace retainers if cracked, warped, loose, or stained.

Replacement Guidelines

  • Clear retainers: Replace every 1-3 years or when damaged
  • Hawley retainers: Can last 5-10 years with proper care
  • Permanent retainers: May last 10-20 years
  • Consider ordering backup sets to avoid treatment gaps

Tip: Consider ordering a backup set of clear retainers to avoid lapse in wear if one is lost or damaged.
