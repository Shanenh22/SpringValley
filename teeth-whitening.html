Professional vs. Over-the-Counter Whitening

Professional teeth whitening uses higher concentrations of bleaching agents (up to 35% hydrogen peroxide) compared to over-the-counter products (typically 3-10%). This means faster, more dramatic results with professional supervision to ensure safety and effectiveness.

Why Choose Professional Whitening?

  • Stronger, more effective bleaching agents
  • Custom-fitted trays for even coverage
  • Professional supervision for safety
  • Faster, more dramatic results
  • Treatment customized to your specific needs
  • Protection for gums and soft tissues

What to Expect During Treatment

In-Office Whitening Process:

  1. Consultation: We'll examine your teeth and discuss your whitening goals
  2. Preparation: We'll clean your teeth and protect your gums with a special barrier
  3. Application: Professional-strength whitening gel is applied to your teeth
  4. Activation: LED light or laser activates the gel for maximum effectiveness
  5. Multiple Cycles: The process is repeated 2-3 times for optimal results
  6. Final Assessment: We'll evaluate your results and provide aftercare instructions

Take-Home Whitening Process:

  1. Impressions: We'll take impressions to create your custom trays
  2. Fitting: Your trays will be fitted for comfort and effectiveness
  3. Instructions: We'll show you how to use the gel and trays properly
  4. Home Treatment: Wear trays as directed
  5. Follow-up: We'll monitor your progress and adjust as needed

Maintaining Your Whiter Smile

To keep your teeth looking their best after whitening treatment, follow these guidelines:

First 48 Hours

Avoid staining foods and drinks like coffee, tea, red wine, and dark berries. Stick to white or clear foods.

Long-term Care

Use whitening toothpaste, drink staining beverages through a straw, and maintain excellent oral hygiene.

Touch-ups

Professional touch-ups every 6-12 months can help maintain your bright smile for years to come.
