What is Gum Disease?

Understanding the stages of gum disease and why early treatment is essential.

Gum Disease Progression

Gum disease begins with gingivitis—red, swollen gums that bleed easily. If left untreated, it can progress to periodontitis, a serious infection that destroys the soft tissue and bone supporting your teeth.

Stage 1: Gingivitis

Early gum inflammation with bleeding and swelling. Reversible with proper treatment.

Stage 2: Early Periodontitis

Bacteria spread below the gum line, causing pockets to form around teeth.

Stage 3: Advanced Periodontitis

Severe infection destroys bone and tissue, leading to tooth loss without treatment.

Act Fast - Early Intervention Saves Teeth

Symptoms of Gum Disease

Recognizing the warning signs helps you get treatment before serious damage occurs.

Early Warning Signs

These symptoms indicate gum disease is developing and requires professional attention.

  • Gums that bleed when brushing or flossing
  • Persistent bad breath or bad taste
  • Swollen, tender or dark red gums
  • Gums that feel tender when touched

Advanced Symptoms

These signs indicate more serious gum disease requiring immediate professional treatment.

  • Receding gumline or longer-looking teeth
  • Loose or shifting teeth
  • Changes in bite or tooth alignment
  • Pus between teeth and gums

Our Periodontal Therapy

Comprehensive treatment approach tailored to your specific gum disease stage.

We begin with a comprehensive gum evaluation to determine the severity of your condition. Treatments often start with deep cleanings (scaling and root planing) to remove plaque and tartar below the gumline.

Deep Cleaning Process

Evaluation

Comprehensive gum assessment with probing depths and X-rays to determine treatment needs.

Scaling

Removes plaque and tartar deposits above and below the gumline using specialized instruments.

Root Planing

Smooths tooth roots to discourage bacteria from reattaching and promote healing.

Antibiotic Therapy

Local antibiotics may be placed to eliminate remaining bacteria and support healing.

Treatment Details

  • Performed under local anesthesia for comfort
  • Treatment sessions typically last 1—2 hours
  • May be split over multiple visits for complex cases
  • Coordination with periodontist for advanced cases

Treatment Safety & Aftercare

Understanding what to expect during recovery and how to maintain your results.

Scaling and root planing are safe and effective, but minor side effects are possible. Good oral hygiene and regular maintenance visits help minimize these risks and support healing.

Possible Temporary Side Effects

  • Temporary bleeding (first 24-48 hours)
  • Minor gum recession as inflammation reduces
  • Tooth sensitivity to temperature
  • Mild tenderness or soreness
  • Slight tooth mobility during healing

Aftercare Instructions

Maintain Results
