Why Regular Dental Cleanings Matter

Regular professional cleanings remove stubborn plaque and tartar that brushing alone can't tackle. Cleanings help prevent tooth decay, gum disease and bad breath while keeping your smile bright. The Cleveland Clinic notes that dental cleanings are essential for optimal oral health and that hygienists remove both plaque and hardened deposits (tartar) that brushing and flossing miss.

How Often Should You Get a Dental Cleaning?

Most people benefit from a professional cleaning about twice a year, but your dentist may recommend a different schedule based on your individual needs. According to the Cleveland Clinic, many patients schedule cleanings every six months, though some with gum disease or a high risk of cavities may require more frequent visits. Children and teens benefit from regular check-ups as well so emerging problems can be addressed early.

Benefits of Routine Teeth Cleanings

  • Reduces your risk of cavities and gum disease: professional cleanings clear away plaque and bacteria that contribute to decay and periodontal disease.
  • Improves your overall health: researchers have linked gum health to heart disease and other systemic conditions, so keeping your mouth healthy can support whole-body wellness.
  • Prevents bad breath: removing tartar and plaque leaves your mouth feeling fresher and cleaner.
  • Saves money by catching problems early: preventive care is less expensive than treating advanced decay or gum disease.

What to Expect at Your Teeth Cleaning Appointment

Your visit will include a thorough examination, gentle cleaning, polishing and personalised oral hygiene tips. Our hygienists make every appointment comfortable and informative.

Our Cleaning Process
